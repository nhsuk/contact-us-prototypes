I would like to complain about an NHS service

The NHS website team can help with questions and feedback about the website. But you can’t give feedback or make a complaint about an NHS service by contacting the NHS website.

If you want to give feedback about an NHS service

You can give feedback on health and social care services in England by -

  • using the service finder to find the place you visited eg, GP practice, hospital and leaving a star rating or review on their profile

You may also be invited to:

  • fill in the Friends and Family Test (FFT) when you complete your treatment or are discharged from a service - if you are not given a form automatically you can ask for one at reception.
  • fill in a Patient Reported Outcomes Measures questionnaire - you may be given this form if you’ve recently had a hip or knee replacement, varicose vein surgery or groin hernia surgery.

Find out more about ways to give feedback

If you want to make a complaint about an NHS service

When making a complaint about an NHS service you can complain to either:

  • the healthcare provider - this is the NHS organisation where you received the NHS service, for example, your GP practice, hospital, dentists or opticians
  • the commissioner - this is the organisation that pays for the service or care you received, for example NHS England, your local clinical commissioning group (CCG)

You have to make the complaint within 12 months of an incident.

You may find it helpful to speak to the Patient Advice and Liaison (PALS) service. You can find a PALS service in most hospitals.

If you are not happy with the way your complaint has been handled you can contact the Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman

For more information

Visit NHS England for more information on how to make a complaint

Or read more on the NHS website about how to give feedback or make a complaint about an NHS service
